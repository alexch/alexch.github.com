Kevin, your general argument, and the entire libertarian movement, would have much more credibility if criticisms of govt intervention were made in the context of a full accounting of history and the real choices at hand.

It is a fact that a quirk of our American system is that companies are the ones that pay for things like health insurance.

And health insurance is extremely expensive.

And companies promised retirement benefits of a certain level to their employees a long time ago.

And there's no govt replacement for those benefits.

Now they're old, and if the company goes under it's the govt that takes on this burden. These people vote, and there's a bit of a moral obligation involved when the govt expects companies to provide for workers, but then big companies go under. You can excuse us fallible humans for not incorporating things like bankruptcy of major corporations into our planning for the future. This is not something humans are good at doing - proven in behavioral economics and cognitive psychology studies.
