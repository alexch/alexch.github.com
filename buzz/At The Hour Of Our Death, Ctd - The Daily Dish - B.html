Ok, so you don't have faith or believe in a conventional concept of a god per se, just an odd way of expressing your positive attitude. Recognizing that there are limits to our knowledge and things beyond our comprehension doesn't really constitute any real belief in a creator, religion, etc., and you appear to be saying that someone else's god is not the same as yours, which is a way of acknowledging that there is no objectively existing god except whatever we each decide to invent.

Also, I hope you won't make the common mistake of many believers when thinking about this stuff, Chad. It doesn't follow that because there are certain things we don't know, that everything (or anything) we imagine is equally likely to be possible.

It's like the difference between thinking as you open your front door "I wonder if I left the living room light on when I left this morning and it's still on right now" and "I wonder if Pete Sampras is doing yoga in my living room right now". One state of the living room is drastically more likely than the other, based on the things we do know. No need to throw out what we do know when thinking about what we don't!
