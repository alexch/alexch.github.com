Jordan Waldrep 108313364266706666206
SS rolls its Treasury Bonds off its balance sheet as they mature and someone else buys them when the Gov reissues.

The funding of the SS Trust fund isn't really that much of a concern as long as people are willing to buy US debt, which they have demonstrated even after the downgrade as seen in the bid to cover ratio of 4.46 in last auction. The whole idea that the Congress and SS Trust Fund are the same thing is fundamentally incorrect as long as people want to buy our bonds.

The concern is if people stop wanting to buy our bonds. Then SS becomes insolvent b/c it is the largest holder by far (19%) and it is running a $45 billion deficit this year. Within that context, one could make a pretty reasonable argument that the deficit debate helps the security of the SS Trust Fund.

All this applies only to the idea of the Trust Fund, which is in no way moot from a funding perspective. With almost $2.7 trillion it can certainly meat its near term obligations. What SS benefits should look like and how to insure that those obligations can be met over the long term are different discussions. Aug 15, 2011 Aug 15, 2011
