To be fair, the study that Minnery cites, probably only included traditional marriages as nuclear families. The study was conducted between 2001-2007. Same-sex marriage wasn't legal in any state until 2004, and in most of the states that it was legal in, it was banned or stopped for one reason or another. Basically, I think it's unlikely that any same-sex marriages were classified as nuclear families, and even if a few were, it wasn't enough to be statistically significant.

The REAL flaw is using this study to justify imposing a loss of freedom on the public. It's not statistically improbable that a certain child raised in a single parent home is going to do better than a different child raised by a nuclear family. It's only unlikely. The numbers in the study show a meager few percentage points in outcomes based on household type. Sure traditional families do best in the study, but so what? Society condones many of the other households types just the same.

The arguments for depriving someone their right to pursue happiness should have a high standard. This study doesn't come close. Jul 24, 2011 Jul 25, 2011
