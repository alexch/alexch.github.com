Relativity predicts that time passes differently in orbit than here on the surface. GPS requires not only the signal about the satellite's position in space but also ephemeris data about precisely when the satellite occupied that location and how long the signal takes to reach the ground, so the receiver can triangulate. Without accounting for the effects of relativity, GPS would be wildly inaccurate. So I think it's a fair chart. Oct 21, 2010 Oct 21, 2010
