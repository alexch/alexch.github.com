Alex Chaffee 107397735779828096052
OK, fair enough, you're not a Tea Partier. Thank goodness! I was worried there for a second :-)

I disagree with "no one will spend your money as effectively and as efficiently as you will" because while that may (or may not) be true at a microeconomics level, it's demonstrably false at a macro level. If I'm commuting to San Jose, it's in my interest to pay for a car and gas given government spending on highways; it's in my interest to pay for a bike and train tickets given government spending on trains. Trains are a more efficient use of scarce resources (fuel, space, time, air quality) and make my life better (I can read the paper or nap on the way to work, and my commute is both shorter and more predictable) but if all I've got to work with is my own money I'll never be able to build a train, so my most efficient choice is the inefficient highway system.

And neither commuter trains nor highways could get built without government involvement to secure the right-of-way and so forth, not to mention the huge initial expense that would be difficult to get VC for since toll roads and train companies can't be flipped in 5 years for 10x.

(It's also demonstrably false at a micro level, since human decision making is seldom rational, but that's a different argument.) Jul 20, 2010 Jul 20, 2010
