Here's my interpretation:

"million moderate march" was a throwaway gag line, chosen because of alliteration and mostly as a reference to Al Sharpton's "million man march". (Cf. http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Million_Man_March#Other_movements_that_based_their_name_on_the_Million_Man_March e.g. Million Mom March)

The whole point of the rally is Reason(ableness) and Sanity, not Moderation per se, but it's hard to tease those concepts apart, especially in a 6-minute comedy monologue. The word "moderate" does not appear on http://www.rallytorestoresanity.com/, but Greenwald and Patrick (at Making Light) are jumping up and kind of overreacting because that word pushed their buttons about a very real and important issue. As someone with a few of those buttons myself, I sympathize with those who rant at people who don't deserve it, because they strayed into a mine field.

Here's Steve Benen's take, which is pretty much the same as mine, and quite (ahem) reasonable: "For what it's worth, my only concern here is one that I often hear in the media -- the notion that the left and right are equally crazy, and the fringes are driving their respective parties' agendas. That strikes me as a mistaken assumption. Republicans really have moved sharply to the far-right and allowed extremists to call the shots, while Truthers and Code Pink have no meaningful influence whatsoever in Democratic politics. But by all appearances, this has nothing to do with partisanship, and everything to do with restoring some sense of sanity to our public discourse. It's a notion I heartily endorse, and hope desperately is successful." Sep 21, 2010 Sep 21, 2010
