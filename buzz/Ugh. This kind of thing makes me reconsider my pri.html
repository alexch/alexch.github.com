Erin S. 110464959902122208399
There is no protected speech whatsoever in a violent act. Even true threats are not protected speech--you have no First Amendment right to call up the guy down the street and tell him you're going to be waiting outside his house with a knife. Stabbing someone you don't like in order to express your dislike of that person is not speech under any conceivable definition, even if it does convey a message. Plus, our criminal law is not just rife with mental state-based distinctions, it is built upon them. Homicide is a different crime depending whether you do it negligently (unaware of a risk), recklessly (aware of and disregarding a risk), willfully (purposefully causing the harm), or with premeditation. In a hate crime, the intent is not only to harm the direct victim but also to harm other people who are like that victim in some way by threatening them with the same. We can debate whether or not the criminal system is able to distinguish between ordinary crimes and hate crimes, and we can debate the merits of additional punishments for hate crimes because of their broader effect, but it's important to be clear about what's going on.
