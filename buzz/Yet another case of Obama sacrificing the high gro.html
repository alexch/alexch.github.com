HCR was only progressive in a relative sense. It curbed the most egregious abuses of a cruel and inefficient system but left the system in place -- in fact, expanded it. When the public option was dropped on the first play, and single payer was dropped before the game began, the country lost the opportunity to see what progressivism really can deliver -- a competitive alternative to laissez-faire (and laissez-mourir) for areas of social good where a pure market solution fails.

Same with the stimulus -- the 40%+ of the less-than-$1T that were devoted to tax cuts didn't get the job done nearly as well as the amounts dedicated to infrastructure and food stamps (etc.). And the Democrats aren't getting even getting credit in public for, what is it, the largest tax cut in US history? Compare this to the WPA and the TVA -- government agencies which directly employed thousands of out-of-work people and created some of the greatest monuments to the idea that government can do good things in the history of the world.

All your other examples are supporting Gerry's point: they're the actions of a competent administration, but don't confuse them with liberalism.
