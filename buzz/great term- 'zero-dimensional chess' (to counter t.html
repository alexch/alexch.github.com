To capitulate is to give up your own goals and take on the goals of your opposition. In every case I can think of, Obama weakened otherwise progressive legislation coming from the Pelosi House (or just left it alone, or didn't mind when the Senate weakened it). And he did it near the beginning of the process, and without securing concessions. That's pre-emptive capitulation.

HCR is only progressive in that it cures the most egregious abuses of a fundamentally unchanged, wasteful, corrupt, inefficient system. And in order to secure votes which never materialized Obama pre-emptively cut out the public option and shut out single payer advocates from the entire process. Medicare For All is progressive; the Health Care Bill That Dare Not Speak Its Name is centrist. It's based on what conservative opponents of HillaryCare proposed 15 years ago. Just because hysterical anti-tax whiners have shifted the debate to the right recently doesn't magically make pro-business centrists into progressives.

The stimulus was enough to stop the bleeding but not enough to cure the wound, or to convince the public that government spending works, so if anything it was anti-progressive.

Hooray for Lily Ledbetter; hooray for Nancy Pelosi. Got anything else? Oct 29, 2010 Oct 29, 2010
