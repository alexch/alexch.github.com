As an anecdotal data point, here are a few reasons I, as a smartphone user (specifically Android 2.2), prefer native apps to webapps.

- Load time. A bookmarked webapp takes at least one HTTP request to start; native apps are nigh-instantaneous. Even on wifi the difference is noticeable and annoying. This may be the single thing that most bothers me about webapps on my phone.

- Running in the background. Having not played with the APIs, I'm not sure to what degree this is supported, but I know that most music-playing sites stop when I put the browser into the background or move to a new window.

- Service integration (including contacts). Once I'm logged into some service (Google, Facebook, Exchange, etc.) any native app with sufficient permissions can access that service without me having to log in again. Webapps require additional authentication, which is a pain. Jul 06, 2010 Jul 06, 2010
